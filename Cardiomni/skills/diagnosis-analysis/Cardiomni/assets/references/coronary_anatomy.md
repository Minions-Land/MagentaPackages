# Coronary Artery Anatomy Reference

## Overview

The coronary arteries supply oxygenated blood to the myocardium (heart muscle). Understanding coronary anatomy is essential for interpreting CTA/DSA images and assessing stenosis clinical significance.

## Major Coronary Arteries

### Right Coronary Artery (RCA)

**Origin**: Right aortic sinus (right coronary cusp)

**Course**: 
- Descends in right atrioventricular (AV) groove
- Curves posteriorly at acute margin
- Terminates at crux of the heart (intersection of AV and interventricular grooves)

**Branches**:
- Conus branch (supplies right ventricular outflow tract)
- SA nodal artery (60% of cases, supplies sinoatrial node)
- Acute marginal branches (supply right ventricular free wall)
- AV nodal artery (90% of cases)
- Posterior descending artery (PDA) in right-dominant circulation (70% of cases)

**Territory supplied**:
- Right ventricle
- Inferior left ventricular wall
- Posterior septum (via PDA in right-dominant)
- SA node (60%)
- AV node (90%)

**Clinical significance**:
- RCA infarction → inferior MI (leads II, III, aVF on ECG)
- Can cause bradycardia/heart block (SA/AV nodal ischemia)

### Left Main Coronary Artery (LM)

**Origin**: Left aortic sinus (left coronary cusp)

**Course**: Short trunk (typically 10-15 mm) that bifurcates into LAD and LCx

**Clinical significance**:
- "Widow maker" - stenosis affects large myocardial territory
- ≥50% stenosis is significant (unlike 70% threshold for other vessels)
- Often requires CABG rather than PCI

### Left Anterior Descending (LAD)

**Origin**: Continuation of left main after bifurcation

**Course**:
- Descends in anterior interventricular sulcus
- Wraps around cardiac apex in many patients
- Longest of coronary arteries

**Branches**:
- Septal perforators (supply anterior 2/3 of interventricular septum)
- Diagonal branches (D1, D2, supply anterolateral LV wall)

**Territory supplied**:
- Anterior left ventricular wall
- Anterior interventricular septum
- Cardiac apex
- Anterolateral wall (via diagonals)

**Clinical significance**:
- LAD infarction → anterior MI (most common, largest territory)
- Proximal LAD occlusion → large anterior MI, high mortality
- "Widow maker" proximal lesions

### Left Circumflex (LCx)

**Origin**: Bifurcation from left main

**Course**:
- Courses posteriorly in left AV groove
- Variable length depending on dominance

**Branches**:
- Obtuse marginal branches (OM1, OM2, supply lateral LV wall)
- Posterolateral branches
- PDA in left-dominant circulation (10%)

**Territory supplied**:
- Lateral left ventricular wall
- Posterior LV wall (in left-dominant)
- SA node (40%)

**Clinical significance**:
- LCx infarction → lateral MI (often "silent" on standard ECG)
- Left-dominant LCx occlusion → posterior MI

## Coronary Dominance

Determined by which artery gives rise to the posterior descending artery (PDA):

- **Right-dominant** (70%): PDA from RCA
- **Left-dominant** (10%): PDA from LCx
- **Co-dominant** (20%): Both contribute to posterior circulation

## AHA 17-Segment Model

Standard nomenclature for coronary segments:

### RCA Segments
1. Proximal RCA (origin to acute margin)
2. Mid RCA (acute margin to origin of PDA)
3. Distal RCA (PDA origin to termination)
4. Posterior descending artery (PDA)
4a. Posterolateral branches

### LAD Segments
5. Proximal LAD (origin to first diagonal)
6. Mid LAD (first diagonal to apex)
7. Distal LAD (apical)
9. First diagonal (D1)
10. Second diagonal (D2)

### LCx Segments
11. Proximal LCx (origin to first OM)
13. Mid LCx (beyond first OM)
15. Distal LCx (terminal branches)
12. First obtuse marginal (OM1)
14. Second obtuse marginal (OM2)
14a. Posterolateral branches

### Left Main
6. Left main trunk

## Anatomical Variants

### Common Variants (Normal)
- Separate origins of LAD and LCx (no left main) - 0.4%
- RCA arising from left sinus - 0.03%
- LCx arising from right sinus - 0.37%
- Myocardial bridging (LAD most common) - 25% on CTA

### Clinical Implications
- Anomalous origin between aorta and pulmonary artery → risk of sudden cardiac death
- Myocardial bridging → systolic compression, may cause ischemia

## Imaging Landmarks

### CTA Identification
- **Aortic root**: Starting point, identify coronary ostia
- **Interventricular grooves**: Follow for LAD and PDA
- **AV grooves**: Follow for RCA and LCx
- **Cardiac apex**: LAD terminus
- **Acute margin** (right heart border): RCA landmark
- **Obtuse margin** (left heart border): LCx/OM landmark

### DSA Projections
Optimal views for each vessel:

**RCA**:
- LAO 30° (visualizes proximal-mid RCA)
- RAO 30° (visualizes distal RCA and PDA)

**LAD**:
- RAO cranial 20-30° (visualizes LAD and diagonals)
- Spider view (steep RAO cranial) for bifurcation

**LCx**:
- RAO caudal 30° (visualizes LCx and OMs)
- LAO cranial (separates LCx from LAD)

## Territory at Risk

Stenosis severity clinical impact depends on territory supplied:

### High-Risk Lesions
- Left main (>50%)
- Proximal LAD (>70%)
- Three-vessel disease (all three major vessels >70%)

### Moderate-Risk Lesions
- Mid LAD or large diagonal
- Proximal LCx or large OM
- Dominant RCA

### Lower-Risk Lesions
- Distal vessel stenoses
- Small branch vessel stenoses
- Non-dominant vessel stenoses
